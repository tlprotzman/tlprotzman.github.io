Interests

High Performance Computing

Going into physics, I quickly realized a field of it which interests me greatly is computational physics, particularly large scale high performance computing. To satisfy my itch to learn more about the field, I started working with Dr. Geidt implementing parallel clustering algorithms for field theories. In my sophomore year, I developed a program implementing the Swendsen-Wang algorithm applied to the Ising model on GPUs, and was able to see a speedup of over 17 times while running on a NVIDIA P100 compared to serial execution. Currently, I am working to apply similar algorithm to a O(4) Phi Four theory, where I expect to see similar if not better performance to the Ising model. I am also working on learning MPI with the intent of developing a many-core implementation as well as further optimizing the GPU version.
